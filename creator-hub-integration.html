Creator Hub Integration

I navigate complex constraints to deliver meaningful outcomes.

The Situation

Skillshare acquired Superpeer a live session and marketplace platform and the directive was immediate: integrate it fast. But Superpeer ran on a separate codebase with its own payment system. A full rebuild wasn't possible in the timeline. And underneath it all, the teacher experience had been neglected for years.

This was a chance to fix two problems at once.

My Advantage

I didn't need to start from scratch on teacher research. Four years of domain knowledge and accumulated pain point data meant I already knew where the experience was broken and what teachers had been asking for. That existing knowledge directly shaped the onboarding flow redesign.

This wasn't a PM-assigned problem. It was something I already knew needed fixing, and the integration created the right moment to address it.

Key Decisions

01 Choose iframe with eyes open

We prototyped the iframe approach with engineering and tested its real limitations before committing. It was the fastest path to integration without a full rebuild. The tradeoff was real less control over the embedded experience but we designed around the constraints rather than pretending they didn't exist.

02 Make it feel like a teacher space, not an admin panel

Teachers spend real time in Creator Hub. The design priority was making it feel considered not bolted on. That distinction mattered to adoption.

Outcome

≈ 0

Teacher complaints post-launch. Integration projects typically generate complaints users notice seams and inconsistencies. This one didn't. Near-zero complaints on a marketplace integration is a metric worth naming.
